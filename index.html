What's up with The Donald?

An analytic tool capturing the latest misinformation trend

I'm a dashboard summarizing what folks are chatting about on The Donald, where many diehard fans of the 45th president congregate. Every few hours, I pull the 500 most popular posts from the landing page on The Donald, crunch some numbers and then show a picture of what they are discussing, so that you don't have to go there.

Election, claims of election fraud trend in the past 30 days

  • This stacked chart compares ESTIMATED claims of election fraud or irregularity against election-related content in each day's posts.
  • Election-related posts were filtered through searching for keywords including "election", "ballot" and "votes."
  • To identify which of these election-related posts contain potential claims of election fraud, a binary classification analysis was conducted with the open-sourced DeBERTa-v3-base-mnli-fever-anli model. (Although the zero-shot classification was not intended to be flawless and include false positives, an volunteer and I had previously analyzed a sample of positive posts and found the macros satisfactory.)

More on claims of election fraud and irregularity

  • The chart on the upper left summarizes location that have been mentioned in posts that also may have also contain claims of election fraud or irregularity. The largest the text, the more frequently a location was mentioned.
  • The table on the right presents posts that may contain claims regarding fraudulent elections. (Once again, please be aware of the potential for individual posts to be false positives.)
